Facts About Angiosperms

Feature Description
Flowers Reproductive structures that attract pollinators like bees, birds, and butterflies.
Seeds Enclosed within fruits, aiding in protection and dispersal.
Vascular System Contains xylem and phloem for water and nutrient transport throughout the plant.
Double Fertilization A unique process in which one sperm fertilizes the egg and another forms endosperm to nourish the embryo.
Fruit The mature ovary of a flower, which encases the seeds and attracts animals for dispersal.
Leaf Diversity Broadleaf structures optimized for photosynthesis; highly variable in shape and function.
Pollination Methods Can be biotic (insects, birds) or abiotic (wind, water), depending on the species.
Global Presence Found in nearly every terrestrial ecosystem, from rainforests to deserts.
